Martin Luther King Jr.

Dr. King

Martin Luther King Jr. (MLK) was a civil rights activists who believed in the liberty for all despite racial differences.
His actions were mainly impacting the African American community in the attempt and success of peacefully protesting to seek liberty.
Dr. King changed the lives of not only the African American community but also of any minority racial and ethnic community.

  • Martin Luther King led the Civil rights movement
  • Led the Montgomery boycott in 1955
  • King graduated from Morehouse College in 1948
  • Martin Luther King delivered the I Have a Dream speech
  • MLK was an American Pastor
